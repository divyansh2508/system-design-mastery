System Design · Module 5 — Caching

Cache Playground

A small cache sits in front of a slow database. Requests for keys stream in, and a few hot keys take most of the traffic. Every request either HITs the cache (fast) or MISSes — fetching from the DB and inserting the key, which forces an eviction when the cache is full. Pick an eviction policy, crank the hot-key skew, and watch the hit ratio move. Who gets evicted is the whole game.

◆ Run these experiments

1

Skew is the free lunch. Drag hot-key skew from flat to high and watch the hit ratio climb — same cache, same policy. Most real traffic is skewed, which is why caching works at all: a few keys carry the load and they all fit.

2

LFU wins on stable popularity. At skew ~1.0, cycle LRU → LFU → FIFO and read the hit ratio. LFU locks the genuinely hottest keys in place, so it posts the highest number when the ranking doesn't change.

3

Watch FIFO betray you. On FIFO, follow the red NEXT OUT badge — it marches down the queue by age, so a red-hot key gets evicted just for arriving early, then instantly re-fetched. That churn is why FIFO trails LRU here.

4

Buy hit ratio with RAM. Add cache slots and the ratio rises toward 100%; remove them and it falls. Capacity and skew both raise hits — latency tracks the ratio directly, since every miss pays the ~80ms DB tax.
